Reading time: 13 min

It's Champions League time once again so we've asked Joris Becq from the Belgian Football Podcast to share a bit of his knowledge and give us the lowdown on our first visitors to St. Petersburg, Club Brugge.

SQUAD

First of all, Brugge’s squad has remained almost unchanged compared to last season. None of the key players left the club, Club Brugge is still relying on the solid foundations that brought them three titles in the past five years, as well as to four participations in the Champions League group stage.

In goal there is Mignolet, the former Sunderland and Liverpool goalkeeper, who has been an excellent solution for Club’s struggles in goal in the years prior. Unfortunately, he won’t be able to feature in this first game because of a positive COVID-test. Horvath will be his replacement, he hasn’t played a game for Brugge in more than a year.

In defence, right-back Mata is probably the most underrated player in the squad. He’s been solid as a rock for them during the last two seasons. Left-back will be Sobol, in central defence Deli, a physically strong guy, usually teams up with Mechele, who is good at positioning. Deli is suspended for this game, and Kossounou, the sole other centre-back in the squad is out (another COVID victim). This causes some issues and either Fuakala will be making his senior debut, or there will be some moving with defensive midfielder Balanta and/or left-back Ricca.

Moving higher up the field, Rits will be cleaning up things behind the experienced central midfield duo Vormer and Vanaken. Vormer can also form a threat in the form of set pieces, with Vanaken frequently at the receiving end. Vanaken is the creative hub, in passing and shooting more than dribbling. Both Vormer and Vanaken often try to get the ball out to both flanks.

At these wings, the danger comes from Diatta, a fast-paced winger with good dribbling skills and with a dangerous strike in his feet. The other dangerous man on the wing is Dennis, he’s definitely also really pacey, but also a bit of a hit-or-miss player. He hasn’t played much this season because of transfer sorrows, and his form doesn’t seem to be there yet either. Chances are that he doesn’t start, in that case, academy prospect De Ketelaere, who made his debut last season in the Champions League and has been involved in most of the games recently, or fresh recruitment Lang are the likely replacements. The striking department is another uncertainty and has been an issue for more than a year now. Lately, powerful Krmenčík seems to have gotten the edge over young Badji, but he’s not available for this game either (the third COVID-positive). If they would play with two strikers, technically gifted Schrijvers is another option.

COACH

Philippe Clement knows all the ins and outs of the club, during his playing career he spent ten years in Brugge. He left the club briefly for spells as head coach at Beveren and Racing Genk, where he won the Belgian title before moving back to Brugge. In his first year he won the title again and had some decent performances at European level. Still a young manager, he’s already been very successful. He seems to prefer a 4-3-3 formation, but a 3-5-2 is used frequently as well. Given the absence of a the latter seems more likely for the game in Saint-Petersburg, covering the defensive setback collectively while trying to counter and using the speed they have higher up the field.

THE SEASON SO FAR & EXPECTATIONS

Last Saturday, Brugge drew 1-1 away in a top game in the Belgian Pro League, away at Standard Liège. Overall, they lost the two of their first three games of the season but have been in excellent form ever since, winning five games in a row before the trip to Liège. This resulted in taking over top spot despite that shaky start. At times, they have looked very poor when trying to build up from the back, if a team presses them high up the field this might cause them trouble. On the other hand, the wingers have been making the difference, this will be a thing Zenit have to watch out for. Brugge obviously are not favourites in this group, but hope to advance in Europe nonetheless, be it in Europa League or in Champions League.
